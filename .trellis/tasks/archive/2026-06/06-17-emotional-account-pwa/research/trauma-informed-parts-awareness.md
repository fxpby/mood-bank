# Trauma-Informed Source And Parts Awareness

## Why This Matters

The product should help users notice that a current trigger may contain both present-moment facts and an older emotional echo. In emotionally activated moments, the current event may feel much larger than its observable facts because it touches earlier experiences of abandonment, shame, humiliation, control, invisibility, inconsistency, or emotional unsafety.

The MVP should support repeated awareness and deliberate small practice during emotional waves. It should not claim to deliver trauma treatment or neural rewiring.

## Source Notes

* SAMHSA's trauma-informed care framework emphasizes safety, trustworthiness, choice, collaboration, empowerment, and resisting re-traumatization. Product implication: source-awareness prompts must be optional, paced, and skippable, especially during high activation.
* The IFS Institute describes Internal Family Systems as a non-pathologizing model in which people have protective and wounded inner parts guided by a core Self. Product implication: MVP can borrow gentle "part noticing" language, especially for inner critic awareness, while not claiming to provide IFS therapy. Source: https://ifs-institute.com/
* The IFS Institute also notes that the evidence base is still developing. Product implication: use "IFS-informed" language conservatively and avoid clinical claims.
* Neuroplasticity is a useful product metaphor for repeated practice and learning, but MVP should avoid claims such as "this app rewires your brain." Product copy should say "反复觉察和小练习会帮助你形成新的回应路径" rather than promising neurological change.

## Product Principles

* Awareness should start from the present: fact, body, emotion, urge.
* Source awareness is optional and should come after stabilization, not before.
* The app should ask "这像不像一种熟悉的旧感觉？" rather than "这是不是童年创伤？"
* Do not push for detailed trauma memories.
* Do not suggest specific abuse, neglect, or childhood events.
* Do not ask the user to relive a traumatic scene.
* Inner critic should be framed as a protective but harsh internal voice, not as the user's true self or an enemy to destroy.
* The user should be able to stop at "not ready to look deeper" and still receive credit for noticing.

## MVP Prompt Direction

Source awareness:

* "这件事里，哪些是今天真的发生的？"
* "哪些像是旧感觉被碰到了？"
* "如果不用讲细节，这像哪一种熟悉的痛？"
* "它在保护我不要再次经历什么？"
* "现在的我能给那个旧感觉一点什么？"

Inner critic awareness:

* "此刻有没有一个很严厉的声音在评价我？"
* "它在说我哪里不够好、太多、太麻烦、太需要、太丢脸？"
* "它可能想防止我经历什么羞耻或受伤？"
* "我能不能先不跟它打架，只和它拉开一点距离？"
* "如果由更稳的我来回应一句，会是什么？"

Practice loop:

1. See the trigger.
2. Separate present facts from old echo.
3. Notice the inner critic or protector strategy.
4. Choose one small new response during the wave.
5. Mark completion later.
6. Save a repetition marker when a similar pattern is noticed again.

## Safety Boundary

MVP should not provide trauma processing, exposure therapy, memory recovery, parts unburdening, diagnosis, or crisis support. It should point users toward professional support when content feels overwhelming, unsafe, dissociative, or connected to self-harm.
